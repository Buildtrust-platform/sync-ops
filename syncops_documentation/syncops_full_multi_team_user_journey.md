# ⭐⭐⭐ **DELIVERABLE 2 — THE FULL MULTI-TEAM SYNCOPS USER JOURNEY** ⭐⭐⭐

**How global teams use SyncOps from idea to archive**

This document narrates the entire production lifecycle from the vantage point of all involved teams.

---

# **PHASE 0 — THE SPARK (Before a Project Exists)**

### **Who is involved:**

* Marketing
* Internal comms
* Creative Director
* Producer
* Exec team

### **What happens:**

Someone says:
“We need a video for the new global announcement.”

### **SyncOps Activity:**

* Producer opens the **Smart Brief** interface.
* SyncOps asks clarifying questions.
* Marketing drops concept notes or a script.
* Bedrock analyzes tone, length, audience, references.
* SyncOps auto-generates:

  * project type
  * estimated duration
  * deliverables list
  * complexity score
  * predicted cost

**End of Phase Output:** A new SyncOps Project with an initial Smart Brief.

---

# **PHASE 1 — INITIATION (Smart Brief to Greenlight)**

### **Primary Actors:**

Producer, Legal, Finance, Creative Director.

---

## **1. Scriptwriter / Creative Director**

* Uploads script, storyboard, mood references.
* SyncOps runs Script-to-Scene Breakdown:

  * scenes
  * locations
  * characters
  * props
  * shot types
  * VFX needs

---

## **2. Legal**

* Sees risk words (Children, Public Transport, Tunnels, Religious Site).
* SyncOps suggests required releases and permits.
* Legal approves or adds additional rules.

---

## **3. Producer**

* Reviews automatic budget estimate.
* Adjusts crew list.
* Confirms deliverables.
* Sends for Finance & Legal review.

---

## **4. Finance**

* SyncOps shows budget tiers based on past similar projects.
* Finance approves or requests revisions.

---

## **5. Legal**

* Reviews rights requirements.
* Identify risk zones.
* Approves safety policies.

---

## **6. SyncOps Greenlight Gate**

Enforces:

* Budget approved
* Legal reviewed
* Insurance valid
* Required permits identified

**Project cannot advance until these are satisfied.**

**End of Phase Output:**
The project is officially **Greenlit**, and the production pipeline unlocks.

---

# **PHASE 2 — PRE-PRODUCTION (Global Planning & Logistics)**

### **Primary Actors:**

Producer, DP/Cameraman, Gaffer, Sound, VFX Supervisor, Studio Ops, Equipment Manager, Legal, Crew.

---

# **2.1 Producer Uses the Logistics Engine**

* Builds shoot days.
* Assigns locations.
* Assigns crew.
* SyncOps checks time-zone conflicts.

## **Field Intelligence Engine activates:**

* Weather risk alerts.
* Local event conflicts.
* Travel time.
* Cultural restrictions.
* Drone legality.
* Political or security concerns.

**Producer gets a Feasibility Score for each shoot day.**

---

# **2.2 Cameraman / DP**

### SyncOps provides:

* Location Policy Brief
* Weather forecasts
* Wind levels for drone
* Sun path diagrams
* Shot list visualizations
* Required gear list extracted from script
* Camera format specs based on deliverables

### DP chooses:

* Camera body
* Lenses
* Frame rate
* Aspect ratio
* Color space

All recorded in the project metadata.

---

# **2.3 Equipment Manager**

Using **Equipment OS**:

* Creates gear package
* Books equipment
* Runs maintenance checks
* Marks unavailable gear
* Recommends alternatives
* Generates packing list

Crew sees gear list in the call sheet.

---

# **2.4 Gaffer / Lighting**

SyncOps automatically tells them:

* Sunrise/sunset
* Golden hour
* Power availability at location
* Weather-related lighting risks

---

# **2.5 Sound Operator**

SyncOps identifies:

* Noise risks
* Audio restrictions (public places)
* Legal consent requirements for interviews
* Recommended mic types

---

# **2.6 Legal**

Before pre-prod ends, legal validates:

* Filming permits
* Location access
* Drone approval
* Talent releases
* Insurance coverage

Any missing item blocks production.

---

# **2.7 Call Sheets (Live)**

Producer publishes:

* Updated call sheets
* Location maps
* Weather conditions
* Transport routes
* Required safety gear
* Crew roles
* Emergency numbers (location-specific)

Call sheet updates trigger SMS/Email.

---

# **End of Pre-Production Output:**

Everything is documented, aligned, permitted, scheduled, and compliant.

---

# **PHASE 3 — PRODUCTION (On Location / Studio)**

### **Primary Actors:**

Crew, DP, Sound, Producer, DIT, VFX Supervisor.

---

## **3.1 Cameraman / DP**

* Uses shot list from SyncOps.
* Confirms scene completion in app.
* Uploads proxy clips if camera-to-cloud is enabled.
* Gets weather warnings in real-time.

---

## **3.2 Sound**

* Uploads scratch tracks or logs issues.
* SyncOps automatically detects audio anomalies.

---

## **3.3 Producer (on or off-site)**

* Monitors crew arrival via check-in.
* Sees weather/location risks live.
* Approves behind-schedule scenes for reshoot.

---

## **3.4 VFX Supervisor**

* Flags shots requiring tracking marks.
* Creates VFX Shot IDs directly in SyncOps.

---

## **3.5 Ingest (DIT or Cloud Proxy)**

All footage must be uploaded through **SyncOps Ingest Tool**:

* Requires Project ID, Camera ID, Shoot Day.
* Auto-renames files.
* Metadata is captured.
* Rekognition and Transcribe run automatically.

Editors get new assets already tagged, searchable, and organized.

---

# **End of Production Output:**

All footage is ingested, tagged, renamed, and compliant.

---

# **PHASE 4 — POST-PRODUCTION (Editorial & Creative)**

### **Primary Actors:**

Editors, Assistant Editors, Colorists, Sound Designers, Motion Graphics, VFX, Producers.

---

## **4.1 Editorial**

Editors get:

* AI-generated selects
* Dialogue search results
* Best take recommendations
* Continuity warnings
* Pre-built scene assembly suggestions

Timeline tasks appear alongside footage:

* “Fix pacing from 00:23–00:27”
* “Legal: needs blur”
* “Producer: remove branded content”

---

## **4.2 VFX Artists**

* See VFX Shot IDs
* Download only necessary plates
* Upload renders back into version stack
* Producer and editor review in the same stack

---

## **4.3 Motion Graphics / Design**

* Pull template from Brand Governance Engine
* SyncOps checks brand compliance
* Autoflag color/font violations
* Upload new graphics with version history

---

## **4.4 Colorist**

SyncOps provides:

* Camera metadata
* Color space
* LUTs
* Scene references
* Export specs for deliverables

Colorists upload final graded masters into the version stack.

---

## **4.5 Sound Designer**

* Accesses proxy audio
* Downloads clean stems
* Uploads mastered sound
* QC flags loudness issues

---

# **End of Post-Production Output:**

A fully reviewed, consistent, technically compliant master exists.

---

# **PHASE 5 — REVIEW & APPROVAL**

### **Primary Actors:**

Internal Reviewers, Clients, Legal, Compliance.

---

## **5.1 Internal Review**

* Time-coded annotations
* Layered comments by department
* AI summary of all comments
* Conflict detection (“Client wants faster pacing, Legal wants slower pacing”)

---

## **5.2 Client Review**

* Only sees approved versions
* Cannot access Rough Cuts
* Expiring secure link
* Watermarked identity
* Comments converted into tasks

---

## **5.3 Legal & Compliance**

Legal uses **Legal Mode**:

* No creative UI
* Only content + associated rights documents
* Approve or deny
* Once approved → *Master becomes Read-Only*

Compliance sees:

* PII detection
* Minor detection
* Drone footage warnings
* GDPR warnings

---

# **End of Review Output:**

Asset is approved, locked, and cleared to distribute.

---

### **Primary Actors:**

### **Primary Actors:**

Archivist, Producer, Data Governance, Finance.

---

## **7.1 SyncOps Archive Automation**

* 30 days after closure, Master → Glacier
* Proxy remains hot
* Search remains active
* Asset Usage Intelligence begins tracking

---

## **7.2 Finance & Executive Dashboard**

SyncOps reveals:

* Cost per finished minute
* Asset ROI
* Clip reuse patterns
* Underused expensive shoots
* Overused footage that needs refresh

---

## **7.3 Project Postmortem (Auto-Generated by AI)**

SyncOps creates:

* What delayed production
* What helped
* What caused reshoots
* Cost overruns & why
* Team productivity summary
